Jarad Anthony Higgins (Juice WRLD)

Chicago rapper and singer who created the emo-rap style

About Juice WRLD

  • 1998 Born on December 2 as Jarad Anthony Higgins in Chicago, Illinois, known professionally as Juice WRLD.

  • 2000 His parents divorced when he was three years old. His father left, leaving his mother to raise him and an older brother as a single parent.

  • 2013 Higgins was a heavy drug user during his childhood and teens. He began drinking lean in sixth grade and using Percocets and Xanax.

  • 2015 Higgins began to develop as an artist in his first year of high school. His first track, "Forever", was released on SoundCloud under the name JuicetheKidd.

  • 2017 "Too Much Cash", his first track to be produced by Nick Mira, was released in 2017. He joined the collective Internet Money and released his debut EP, 9 9 9.

  • 2018 "Lucid Dreams" was released as a single and peaked at number two on the Hot 100, becoming one of the most streamed songs of 2018.

  • 2019 Higgins's second studio album, Death Race for Love, was released and topped the Billboard 200 chart. He toured with Nicki Minaj.

  • 2020 Higgins appeared posthumously on Eminem's "Godzilla" and the family confirmed the release of his unfinished work after his tragic passing.
